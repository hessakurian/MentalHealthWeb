Around the world, people have different outlooks on mental health. It is common for mental health to not be taken as a serious matter. This creates a stigma around mental health, which makes it difficult for individuals to seek professional help. The purpose of this website is to raise awareness about the importance of easy access to mental health care and the dangers of stigma.

What is Mental Health Stigma?

Stigma in mental health refers to negative beliefs about a person struggling with their mental health. The person can be treated differently, given an unfair label, or forced to feel embarrassed (healthdirect, 2023).

Why does Mental Health Stigma exist?

These types of beliefs come from ignorance, prejudice, and misinformation (healthdirect, 2023).
According to the World Health Organization (WHO), mental health systems, around the world, are significantly under-resourced and the treatments are often “poor in quality.” Moreover, in 2019, 970 million people were dealing with anxiety and depression globally. Not receiving proper mental health care can worsen an individual's well-being and create problems in their relationships, schools, and workplaces.
A study from the Cohen Veterans Network and the National Council for Mental Health Wellbeing reveals that 31% of Americans are worried about telling others about receiving professional help as they fear judgment and ridicule. According to healthdirect, dealing with stigma can affect an individual’s self-esteem and can trigger suicidal thoughts.
